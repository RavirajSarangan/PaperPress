How PDF to Word conversion works

A PDF doesn’t contain paragraphs — it contains thousands of individually positioned text fragments. PaperPress groups fragments into lines by their vertical coordinates, orders them left to right, then measures the spacing between lines: a gap larger than the page’s typical line height marks a paragraph break. Those paragraphs are written into a standards-compliant Office Open XML package, which is what a .docx file actually is. Everything happens locally, so confidential reports and contracts stay on your machine.

Frequently asked questions

Will the Word file match the PDF exactly?

You get all the text in clean, editable paragraphs. Pixel-perfect layout recovery is impossible for any converter — a PDF stores positioned characters, not document structure — so complex designs are simplified but fully editable.

What about scanned PDFs?

Scanned PDFs are photographs of pages with no text layer. PaperPress detects this and points you to the OCR tool, which reads the scan in English, Tamil, or Sinhala.
